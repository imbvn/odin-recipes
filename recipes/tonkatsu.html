Tonkatsu (Japanese Pork Cutlet)

Description

Crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside, this homemade Tonkatsu (Japanese Pork Cutlet) is my favorite! I‘ll show you the secret to an airy breading that makes a light and supercrisp coating. Serve it with my tangy sesame dipping sauce and a refreshing shredded cabbage salad for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients

Sesame Dipping Sauce
  • 1 tbsp toasted white sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp toasted black sesame seeds
  • 4 tbsp tonkatsu sauce
Breading
  • 1/2 cup panko
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
Tonkatsu
  • 2 boneless pork loin chops
  • 1/2 tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt
  • 1/8 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 cups neutral oil

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Grind the white and black sesame seeds.
  3. Put the sesame seeds divided in a dipping bowl along with tonkatsu sauce. Mix when you are ready to eat.
  4. Prepare the breading ingredients. Crack 1 large egg in a deep dish and add 1/2 tbsp neutral oil. Whisk together until well combined. Then, prepare another deep dish with 2 tbsp all-purpose flour.
  5. Use a sharp knife to remove and discard the extra fat from your pork loin chops. Then, make several slits on the connective tissue between the meat and fat.
  6. Flip the meat and make several slits on the other side of the connective tissue.
  7. Pound both sides of the meat with the back of a knife or a meat tenderizer/mallet.
  8. Mold the meat back into its original shape with your hands.
  9. Season both sides of the meat with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  10. Dredge the pork in the flour and dust off excess. Next, dip the pork into the egg mixture and coat well. Finally, dredge the pork in the fresh panko, gently pressing the panko onto the pork. Set aside for 5-10 minutes.
  11. Add neutral oil to a pot and bring it to 340ºF (170ºC) over medium heat.
  12. Gently put your pork into the oil and cook for 1 minute. Don't touch the pork for 30 seconds.
  13. After a minute, flip the pork and cook the other side for 1 minute.
  14. Take out the pork and remove excess oil. Let it sit for 4 minutes.
  15. Fry your piece of pork a second time in 355ºF (180ºC) for 30 seconds on each side. Let it rest for 2 minutes.
  16. Serve how you'd like. Traditionally, it is sliced and served with cabbage.
